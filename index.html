About & Research Focus

I am a Ph.D. student in Brain, Mind, and Computer Science at the University of Padua, supported by a research grant from Fondazione Bruno Kessler (FBK). I hold an M.Sc. in Artificial Intelligence (2023, Padua) and previously worked on deep learning for EEG-based epilepsy prediction and large language models for cognitive analysis at Ca’ Foscari University of Venice.

My current doctoral research focuses on AI-driven prediction and management of chronic diseases, particularly Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD). By combining machine learning, human-centered design, and psychological insights, I develop digital health tools that can identify at-risk patients and support personalized prevention and self-management strategies.

I am also collaborating with Dr. Cisotto from the University of Trieste on the evaluation of hvEEGNet, a novel model for EEG signal reconstruction. In this project, we investigate the model’s robustness to noise and artifacts following the detection of anomalies in earlier experiments. The collaboration aims to better understand the origin of these inconsistencies and to validate hvEEGNet’s reliability, with plans for a joint publication on its performance and diagnostic applications.

Beyond my technical work, I am passionate about bridging artificial intelligence and neuroscience to improve healthcare accessibility and patient outcomes. I collaborate with research groups across Europe on projects in brain–computer interaction and digital medicine, aiming to design AI systems that make a tangible impact on people’s well-being.
